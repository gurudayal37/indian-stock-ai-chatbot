Methodology & Calculations

Historical Average Method
  • Expected Earnings: Average EPS of last 4 quarters
  • Actual Earnings: Current quarter EPS
  • Unexpected Earnings: Actual - Expected
  • SUE Formula: Unexpected Earnings / Standard Deviation
Trading Rules
  • High SUE (>1.0): BUY signal
  • Low SUE (<-1.0): SELL signal
  • Medium SUE (-1.0 to 1.0): HOLD
  • Hold Period: Up to 15 days or 2% stop loss

Research Foundation

"An Empirical Evaluation of Accounting Income Numbers"

Authors: Ray Ball & Philip Brown, 1968

Key Finding: Stock prices continue to drift in the direction of earnings surprises for several weeks after earnings announcements, creating profitable trading opportunities.

PEAD Anomaly: This market inefficiency allows traders to profit from the delayed price adjustment to earnings information.
