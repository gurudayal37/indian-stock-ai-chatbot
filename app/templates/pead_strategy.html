PEAD Strategy

Post-Earnings Announcement Drift - Academic Research Based Trading

Based on the seminal 1968 research by Ball & Brown

Research Foundation

"An Empirical Evaluation of Accounting Income Numbers"

Authors: Ray Ball & Philip Brown, 1968

Journal: Journal of Accounting Research

This groundbreaking study established the foundation for earnings-based trading strategies and the PEAD anomaly.

Strategy Overview

The Post-Earnings Announcement Drift (PEAD) is a well-documented market anomaly where stock prices continue to drift in the direction of earnings surprises for several weeks after earnings announcements.

Positive Surprise

When actual earnings exceed expectations, stocks tend to continue rising for 2-6 weeks.

Negative Surprise

When actual earnings fall short of expectations, stocks tend to continue declining for 2-6 weeks.

Methodology & Calculations

Method 1: Historical Average Approach
Expected Earnings Calculation:

Expected Earnings = Average EPS of previous 4 years

Standard Deviation:

Standard deviation of the last four years earnings

Method 2: Analyst Expectations Approach
Expected Earnings:

Average of analyst expectations

Standard Deviation:

Standard deviation of analyst expectations

Standardized Unexpected Earnings (SUE) Formula
SUE = (Actual Earnings - Expected Earnings) / Standard Deviation

Where:

  • Actual Earnings: Reported EPS for the quarter
  • Expected Earnings: Either historical average or analyst consensus
  • Standard Deviation: Volatility measure of earnings expectations

Trading Rules

Buy Signals
  • High SUE: SUE > +1.0 (Strong positive surprise)
  • Moderate SUE: SUE > +0.5 (Moderate positive surprise)
  • Action: Buy the stock immediately after earnings announcement
Short Signals
  • Low SUE: SUE < -1.0 (Strong negative surprise)
  • Moderate SUE: SUE < -0.5 (Moderate negative surprise)
  • Action: Short the stock immediately after earnings announcement
Position Management
  • Entry: As soon as results are announced (within 1 hour)
  • Hold Period: Maximum 15 days (2 weeks)
  • Stop Loss: 2% of total portfolio value
  • Exit: Take profit or stop loss, whichever comes first

Risk Management

Market Risk

PEAD works best in efficient markets. Market crashes can override earnings effects.

Liquidity Risk

Ensure sufficient trading volume for quick entry/exit positions.

Position Sizing

Never risk more than 2% of portfolio on a single PEAD trade.
